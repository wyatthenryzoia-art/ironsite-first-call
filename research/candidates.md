# Candidates considered

## Picked: Limbach Holdings (NASDAQ: LMB)

Public mechanical contractor, ~$650-680M 2025 revenue guidance, ODR model focused on building systems that have to stay running (healthcare, industrial, data centers). Union craft workforce. Explicit T&M and labor-utilization language in public filings. New EVP (Angerosa) in an ops-facing national role as of Jan 13, 2026.

Why picked: citation density. Every claim about this company can be tied to a dated earnings call, 10-K, or press release, which is Max's bar. T&M maps directly to Ironsite's wedge.

## Rejected: Rosendin Electric

~$4B revenue, largest employee-owned electrical contractor, ESOP since 2000. New CEO as of Jan 2025 (Mike Greenawalt retired). Big logo, ESOP story is genuinely a good fit for per-worker productivity data.

Why cut: newsroom page returned "no matches" on fetch. Private company so no earnings calls or 10-K to anchor dated signals. Building a credible briefing would require phone calls I can't make.

## Rejected: Faith Technologies

Top 50 specialty contractor per ENR, ~1,000-5,000 employees, expanding to El Paso, Excellerate modular division grew 438K sq ft in 2024. Private.

Why cut: the recent signals I could find (El Paso expansion, modular facility growth) are about capital deployment, not labor productivity or T&M pain. Weaker alignment with Ironsite's wedge.

## Rejected: Southland Industries

ENR #13 overall, #3 mechanical, largest privately-held mechanical firm. Partnered with ServiceTitan in June 2024 on service ops.

Why cut: ServiceTitan partnership predates the 12-month signal window and already covers some of Ironsite's surface area (service/T&M workflow). A briefing would need to explain why Ironsite layers on top of an existing ServiceTitan deployment, and that is a harder first call.

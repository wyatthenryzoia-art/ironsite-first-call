# Productivity baselines used in the mock dashboard

All percentage ranges in the Section 4 mock are anchored to published construction research. Each range is cited inline in the HTML.

## Wrench time (direct install)

- **Range used in mock:** 25-35% of shift.
- **Primary source:** McKinsey Global Institute, "Reinventing Construction: A Route to Higher Productivity" (Feb 2017). Study reports ~30% direct wrench time for a typical journeyman day.
- **URL:** https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/reinventing-construction-through-a-productivity-revolution
- **Caveat:** 2017 study is still the most widely cited field-productivity benchmark. Nothing more recent at the same methodology depth has replaced it.

## Travel / material staging / prep

- **Range used in mock:** 35-45% of shift.
- **Source:** Same McKinsey study. Approximately 40% of a craft workday is spent on preparation, gathering equipment and materials, and transitions between work areas.

## Rework

- **Range used in mock:** 5-10% of shift.
- **Source:** FMI Corporation, Construction Industry Institute research on rework cost. FMI puts rework at ~5% of contract value in self-perform trades, translating to roughly 5-10% of field-labor time depending on scope.
- **Caveat:** Presented as a range, not a point estimate.

## Wait on material / crew coordination

- **Range used in mock:** 10-20% combined.
- **Source:** Dodge Data & Analytics SmartMarket reports on jobsite productivity; KPMG global construction surveys. Both cite waiting and coordination as the second largest field-time waste bucket after travel/prep.

## Macro anchor

- **Construction labor-productivity growth:** averaged ~1% per year over the past two decades, vs. ~3.6% in manufacturing. (McKinsey, same report.)
- **Use in mock:** footnote, not headline.
